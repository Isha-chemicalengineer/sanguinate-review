# SANGUINATE® (PP-007) – Hemoglobin Based Oxygen Carrier Review
## Overview
This project explores SANGUINATE® (PP-007), a PEGylated bovine carboxyhemoglobin developed as a Hemoglobin-Based Oxygen Carrier (HBOC) and artificial blood substitute.
The review focuses on:
- Oxygen transport mechanisms
- PEGylation and protein engineering
- Carbon monoxide mediated vasodilation
- Clinical trials and therapeutic applications
- Market potential and future prospects
## Key Topics Covered
- Artificial blood substitutes
- Oxygen delivery systems
- PEGylated hemoglobin
- Sickle Cell Disease applications
- Ischemia-reperfusion injury
- Vacuum and vascular physiology
- HBOC safety and toxicity
## Major Areas Discussed
### Preparation Method
- Hemoglobin purification
- Carboxyhemoglobin formation
- PEGylation
- Sterile pharmaceutical formulation
### Chemical & Biophysical Properties
- Oxygen affinity
- Pharmacokinetics
- CO release mechanisms
- Oxidative stress reduction
### Clinical Research
- Sickle Cell Disease
- Acute ischemic stroke
- End-stage renal disease
- Trauma and emergency medicine
